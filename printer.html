3D PrinterEnclosure

Personal Project

I love 3D design and 3D printing is an awesome way of getting from a concept to a tangible part or device. However, 3D printing is a smelly business and the fumes can be toxic, especially when pringing TPU or ABS. I live in a small apartment in a cold country, so ventilation can't solve the problem. This is why I decided to build an enclosure with HEPA and active carbon fume extraction. The enclosure also helps to keep the ambient temperature steady, improving print quality.

For the frame of the enclosure, I bought a server cabinet cheaply online and insulated it with recycled insulation sheets, and covered the insulation with. Aluminium foil to maximize heat reflection. The sheets of insulation covered with aluminium where bolted onto the enclosure walls and the edges were further sealed with aluminium tape. Finally, I placed a garden stone on the floor of the enclosure to minimize vibration from the floor.

I also needed to build the fans and filtration system to collect fumes from the enclore, for this I used two 12V computer fans, ventilation parts and 3D printed fittings. I inserted the HEPA filter on the outside of the filtration system and sheets of active carbon filters on the inside. Finally this system was sealed with hot glue to prevent any air leakage. The enclosure door was sealed with window seal.

In order to keep the electronic components from failing in the relatively high ambient temperature, I extended all the electrical wires and moved the electronics on the outside of the enclosure. The only electronic parts inside the enclosure are the stepper motors, limit switches and LED-lighting.

I also designed a new filament spool holder, that I could keep dry and in room temperature outside the enclosure. The enclosure is not yet finished, and as soon as I find the time, I will create an Arduino-based overheat shutdown system and a Raspberry-based remote control and monitoring system.
