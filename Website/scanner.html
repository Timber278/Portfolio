NIR SpectrometryScanner

Product Development Thesis

I am currently working on my thesis, which is a product development project to develop a scanner to be used at plastics recycling locations to identify different types of plastics. The project includes 3D-design of injection molded parts and selection of electronics components.

On the component side the scanner runs on Raspberry Pi 4 and includes a uninterruptible power supple (UPS), which enables the scanner to be used on battery power and while charging via USB-C. Sensor used is a NIR-spectrometry sensor inserted in a custom-made housing support.

All electronic components are housed inside the main compartment shown in the images above. The handle contains the batteries, connection wires, trigger used for scanning, charging cable and a water-tight plug that holds the batteries in place and closes the electrical circuit.

All of the plastic components have been designed in SolidWorks and 3D-printed on my modified Creality Ender-3 with PLA. The scanner will undergo testing, after which I will design a final version.
