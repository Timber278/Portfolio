About Me

- Enthusiastic, innovative, creative -

I feel passionately about engineering and science. By nature I get excited about things that most people don't care about and look to solve problems that might not even exist. While I study mechanical engineering, I also spend a lot of my freetime on personal projects and tinkering, as well as learning math and science. Sometimes I feel like there aren't enough hours in a day or days in a year to work on all the things I'm interested in.

I will graduate in April or May 2021. My studies are focused on mechanical design, mechatronics and automation. Currently I'm working on a product development project for my thesis, finishing up my studies and looking for a summer internship or permanent position in mechanical engineering, with a particular interest in mechanical design and product development.

I love taking on new challenges and tackling problems, especially ones that don't already have a clear-cut answer, as this leaves room for creative problem solving and coming up with unique outside the box solutions. At first I always dread the complexity and work-load of what I'm about to undertake, but step by step the vision gets clearer and the job gets easier.
